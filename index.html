Bratislava is the political, cultural and economic centre of Slovakia. It is the seat of the Slovak president, the parliament and the Slovak Executive. It is home to several universities, museums, theatres, galleries and other important cultural and educational institutions.[5] Many of Slovakia's large businesses and financial institutions also have headquarters there.

The history of the city has been strongly influenced by people of different nations and religions, namely by Austrians, Croats, Czechs, Germans, Hungarians, Jews, Serbs[6] and Slovaks (in alphabetical order).[7] The city was the capital of the Kingdom of Hungary, a part of the larger Habsburg Monarchy territories,[8] from 1536 to 1783 and has been home to many Slovak, Hungarian and German historical figures.
